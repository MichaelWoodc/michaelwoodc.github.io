2022-2023 Candidates

Executive Officer Positions

President

I have valued my experiences as a Senator and in the I have served the student body through several roles, and now as the current President. I’ve noticed when it comes to advocating for the student body, the work is never done. Within this academic year alone, students have witnessed several shootings, discrimination, mental health instability, and a lack of guidance on how to contact those who can enable change at Georgia Southern. If given the opportunity to serve #ONEMORETIME, as your SGA President, my main focus will be campus safety, continuous efforts for inclusivity, mental health awareness, and accessibility. Eagle Nation deserves a President who will leave the University better than when they started.

Campaign Platform:

Mental Health Awareness: Amidst the Pandemic, and the transition into the new “normal” there has been a surge of mental health crisis arising from the college population. I was appointed by the USG Acting Chancellor, to serve as a student representative for the USG Mental Health Consortium to speak on behalf of the students and what USG can do to better serve the students of the 26 institutions in Georgia.

If re-elected: – I will ensure that SGA will have events monthly or semesterly to give students events to allow them to take a day to relax from any stress they may have. – I will work with the Legislative Branch (SGA Senate) to put forth legislation to dedicate 1-2 days of each semester to be recognized by the University as mental health awareness days. Campus Safety: This academic year students at Georgia Southern who live on campus have experienced many alerts regarding shootings and threats on campus. As a result, students have reported not feeling safe on campus and not wanting to return.

If re-elected: – I will work with On Campus Housing, University PD, and University Administration to host semesterly forums for students to attend to gain insight on what’s going on in and around campus. This will allow for students to voice their concerns directly and have open discussion in regards to safety. – I will work with the University to implement a gate system for all dorm halls to only allow access for students to get into their on campus housing. – I will work with the University to implement an Eagle ID (key) system for entry into academic buildings during University operating hours. This will be to only allow students, faculty and staff qualified to enter our buildings of study. Inclusivity: After listening to the concerns of students at a Minority student event, it is clear that there are still problems arising on campus surrounding inclusion. Furthermore, it is evident that many students are unaware of the resources that are available for use on campus revolving around diversity and inclusion.

If re-elected: – I will ensure that SGA hosts the Eagles United: Multicultural Event on each campus so all students of Georgia Southern can experience different cultures through knowledge, food, and entertainment. – I will ensure that all members of SGA are trained in accordance with the University in Diversity and Inclusion. This will allow for all members of SGA to be of assistance to all students in matters involving discrimination of any sort. Furthermore, it will allow members of SGA to be a great resource to contact when it comes to such matters. – I will ensure that SGA participates in more diversity activities and host discussions with other departments of the University such as, Office of Multicultural Affairs (OMA), to learn more about the issues that are happening for students on campus to share with University administration. – I will work with the SGA Public Relations Coordinator to advertise resources available from the Office of Inclusive Excellence (OIE) and Office of Multicultural Affairs (OMA) Accessibility: Students are unaware of who they can contact or talk to when they have a concern or in need of assistance.

If re-elected: – I will work with members of SGA to host quarterly meetings with the student body to advise them on what resources SGA offers such as Eagles for Eagles, Gus’s Garments, our partnership with the Food Pantry. This meeting will also consist of various members from the University administration who will highlight hot topics that students would like to discuss.

Hey Eagles! I’m Brianne—a Pre-Law Public Health major. As someone actively involved on campus and a member of SGA, I take the work that affects our students as a great responsibility. With being an executive board member for four separate student organizations, my involvement in a service sorority, and working with students across Southern I have learned leadership and interpersonal skills that I believe will help me lead SGA if I was given the opportunity. I want to work to shape SGA as a hub for community and resources while creating an inclusive, accessible environment and prioritizing our students’ health.

Campaign Platform:

Shaping SGA as a hub for Community and Resources-One of Southern’s biggest struggles is being able to reach students with the resources that are available for them. I frequently see students struggling with things that could easily be helped by resources already available to them on campus. If elected, I would make it a priority to connect students to these resources and create a resource book for all three campuses that answered FAQs and listed different resources for different concerns. This would also require creating an environment in SGA that would be helpful and welcoming to all students who wish to visit. I would love to prioritize drop-in events throughout the semester to connect students and make them feel welcome in the office and connect to SGA in different ways. As president I would also pledge to be active with the members of Senate and Cabinet, helping every way I can to uplift the Senate and Cabinet to achieve their goals. I hope to create an environment that allows everyone to thrive as much as possible, from members of SGA to the entire Student Body.

Creating an Inclusive & Accessible Environment-Georgia Southern has made many strides in the last few years towards becoming a place that is more welcoming and accepting of people from diverse backgrounds. But it is no secret that there are many ways that we need to improve; from recognizing microaggressions to combating blatant racism, homophobia, and ableism that many of our students deal with daily. We need to push accountability and education. This would include events throughout the school year that educate students about the diversity on campus, how to interact with people from different cultures, and how to hold each other accountable for our actions. I would also push for more forms of communication, marketing, and awareness to help all students take advantage of all the programs and initiatives that are currently at GSU. I think one of the biggest issues we see is not only having the resources for students, but helping students identify the resources we do have. Accessibility is also a huge concern I know me and many others have on campus on a daily basis-and many issues that need to be addressed to make Southern a place that everyone can enjoy, regardless of ability.

Prioritizing our Students Health and Wellness-The physical, mental, and social health and safety of the students at Georgia Southern should be our number one priority. As President of SGA, I would work to make sure that the well-being of students is prioritized in all decision-making and help uplift students to make decisions that are best for their health as well.

Executive Vice President

Campaign Platform:

My name is Audrey Lowery. I was a Senator for the College of Engineering and Computing my sophomore year. I love serving my university, as it has given so much to me, so I wanted to give back to its students. I am running for the position of Executive Vice President. I want to make sure the students of Georgia Southern are heard, and have a safe and fun experience at Georgia Southern University.

1. I want to create a safe space for students who are suffering from suicidal thoughts, and I want to bring awareness through Suicide Awareness week. I have seen some of my friends pass this way, which is why it is an important topic to me.

2. I want to try to create posters and signs to put up around campus with facts about recycling and littering. I also want to create community service groups to go around campus to pick up trash. As I walk to class every day, I see trash along side the road, and I want to try to clean up campus, to “Keep GSU Beautiful”.

I am running for Executive Vice President of SGA because I believe that the students of Georgia Southern need someone who is honest and will tell the truth when it comes to important issues facing students on campus no matter how hard the truth may be, someone who will be committed entirely to serving our student body, someone who has the experience within SGA and on campus in general to serve and bring about much needed change that I believe that Georgia Southern students need and want, and someone who has the compassion to listen to students and then figure out a way to satisfy their needs.

Campaign Platform:

The issues that face Georgia Southern University at the present moment are:

1. Campus Safety: Safety at Georgia Southern has been a major issue since I’ve been attending classes and have been a member of SGA. We’ve had several incidents of campus shootings at our residential facilities such as one that occurred last semester at Kennedy Hall and more recently at Freedoms Landing. As your Executive Vice President and as a fellow student, the safety of our students will be and is a top priority for myself and my running mate, President Belvin. I plan, if elected, to join President Belvin in having a conversation with university officials on what we can do to make sure that students who reside at our residence halls feel safe as they call Southern their home away from home. Another safety concern, that was brought to my attention was that our campus is too accessible to outside individuals and therefore creates what I believe a “soft target”. I am proposing that all academic buildings only be accessible to students, faculty, and staff by them using their Eagle IDs to access such buildings to make sure that the general public cannot just have access to areas where our students are without having to go through a secure process such as verifying ID and reason before being allowed through the doors.

2. Cultural Competency: I believe that we need to do more as a university to instill cultural competency in all of our students. For example, students need to be educated on how they should treat those plots that are reserved for NPHC. Many of them do not understand that it is considered disrespectful to sit or stand on those plots and it has been brought to my attention that some members of the NPHC feel that individuals are deliberately doing those things. As EVP, I want to get something in place, where those plots are labeled so there is no confusion as to who they belong too. Also, I have observed some individuals wearing clothing items that many members of the African American community find offensive, but those individuals are not aware because it wasn’t explained to them. We need to incorporate a component in our FYE classes on cultural competency so incoming students will already be taught about certain behaviors and how they make people of color feel especially attending a PWI where we are already in the minority. I believe that many in the African American community on campus feel that more can be done in order to make us feel comfortable on campus just like our non-black counterparts. My goal as EVP is to get Georgia Southern to a place where everyone feels that they are on an equal playing field and not being discriminated against because they are a part of a marginalized group. I want to work with the Office of Inclusive Excellence to develop more programs to work with our African American and Hispanic students and make them feel like they are welcomed and that they will not be treated differently.

Vice President of Finance

My duty as Vice President of Finance this past year was to oversee the organization’s day-to-day financial management. Funds are requested by students and organizations for forthcoming conferences and events. We were able to increase the funding amount for individuals who requested funds for a conference in my function. If elected to this position, I intend to carry on my tradition of serving Georgia Southern students. Getting rid of financial barriers so that all students have equal opportunity for professional and educational advancement. If elected as Vice President of Finance, I intend to continue to maximize the organization’s budget and strategize to ensure the continuous success of The Student Government Association.

Campaign Platform:

1.) Student mental health A college student’s academic performance and achievement might be impacted by mental health issues. Poor mental health can lead to a lack of motivation and difficulty focusing, which can result in poor grades. Students seeking support from campus-wide mental health resources could be the key to their academic success. As SGA, we can collaborate with various campus resources to ensure that every student is aware of and has the opportunity to utilize the resources available here at Georgia Southern.

2.) Diversity and Inclusion The educational experience is enhanced by diversity. We learn from people who have different experiences, ideas, and viewpoints than us, and the greatest way to teach these lessons is in a richly diversified intellectual and social setting. It encourages personal development as well as a healthy campus environment. SGA can help bring awareness by putting on events, and conversing with university officials about the struggles we may see on campus to bring about the change needed at Georgia Southern University !

Vice President of Auxillary Affairs

Hello Eagle Nation! I am seeking the position of Vice President of Auxilliary Affairs because I want to be a voice in amplifying our college experience. I am a past SOAR Leader, the current PR Coordinator of SGA, and an active leader on campus, so I know all about the Auxiliary Services on our campus. As a member of SGA, I will strive to be a voice for the underrepresented. As a first-generation college student and minority, I know firsthand the importance of inclusion and diversity. I believe that representation is an important factor when it comes to decision-making. Over the past few years, with dreams of one day becoming a lawyer, I have come to realize that my purpose is to serve my community and mentor others. With this being said, I have had a plethora of experiences that will allow me to represent you in the best way possible. As the Vice President of Auxilliary Affairs, I will strive to make an impact, not just an impression.

Campaign Platform:

1) More Convenient Parking and Transportation: I will like students to have the ability to park in more lots and spaces on campus, without penalty. In my position, I hope to develop something that will allow more ticket appeals. Over four years, an individual’s financial state can change and one may not be able to pay off a citation. I want to see these issues and more tackled, such as addressing why there isn’t free library parking during the day before the employee parking becomes available.

2) Tastier Food and More Options: As a freshman who loved the dining commons, it’s been hard seeing the decline in popularity. I want us to love our cafeteria. COVID or not, we want to love what we eat and I hope to help dining see that.

3) A More Personalized Housing Portal: I remember being a freshman and using the system. I had to put my social media in my message at the end, all because we weren’t able to add photos. Who we are and our uniqueness matter! I hope to work with housing to create a more personalized housing experience. Also, I hope to dive into how to better roommate matching.

To be a friend and family to each and every student and for you all to 100% know that you can count on me to get the job done. Georgia Southern has already given me so much and I hope to be able to give back all of that and then some.

Campaign Platform:

Diversity and Inclusion ⁃ Diversity and inclusion means being a good team player. It means not only acknowledging our differences in race, gender, sexuality, etc, but being mature and respectful. To operate on the basic principle of treat others how you want to be treated and for us to all have spaces where we feel that we belong in and have people that support us. While we already have student ran events and forums that focus on this, I feel as though there not wide broadcasted. If you weren’t in the org that’s hosting or happen to see a flier or two you completely miss out. There is great events and information being provided but I think that students often miss them.

Financial Disparities ⁃ As a first generation college student I know first hand about financial hardship. Finances is the make or break to whether students continue with their education. While this is a university and ultimately a business, it’s students that keep them in business. Outside of the eagles for eagles fund and one or two other initiatives. There isn’t any other last minute funding options for emergencies that may arise for students. This is something I would like to see addressed.

Through each of these steps I believe that SGA can without a doubt take the foundation of Alumni Relations to the next level. With having the intake of the undergrad student body, and experience of alumni, we can work together to the create a suitable post graduate experience that offer multiple opportunities and networks of support.
